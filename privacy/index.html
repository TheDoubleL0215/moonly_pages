To comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) requirements for international transfers (Chapter V), we ensure your data receives EU-equivalent protection through the following legal mechanisms:

  • Adequacy Decisions & The EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework (DPF): Google LLC is certified under the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework. The European Commission has determined that the DPF provides an adequate level of protection for personal data transferred from the EU to participating companies in the United States.
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): In instances where the DPF does not apply, or for transfers to other Google infrastructure outside the EEA, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission. These legally binding agreements ensure that Google processes your data with safeguards matching European privacy standards.

6. Your Privacy Rights (GDPR)

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to Access: You can request a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Right to Portability: You can export your health logs and data in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): You can delete your account and all associated health data directly within the application settings at any time.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: You may revoke your consent for health data processing at any time. Doing so will immediately cease tracking and delete past health logs from our active servers.
  • Right to Rectification: The right to correct inaccurate or incomplete personal and health data.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing: The right to request a temporary halt on processing your data under certain circumstances (e.g., if you are contesting the accuracy of the data).
